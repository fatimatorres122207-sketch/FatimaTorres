My Passion!

I've always loved otome games. These are story-based video games were players can control a protagonist who forms romantic realationships with male characters through visual storytelling, dialogue, and player choices that influence the narrative and lead to different endings. I loved playing the indie versions on Itch.io! There I discover game delevloper logs where they talked about the progess of the game. I found out they made them through Renpy that uses phython to create those types of games. I decided to try making a game for myself and I really enjoyed it. I now try to learn as much code as I can to be able to create a small comapny to create otome games!

Jefferson High School Coding Club

  • Founded and led a Coding Club at Jefferson High School, fostering an inclusive environment that catered to beginner, intermediate, and advanced skill levels within a diverse student body spanning various ethnicities and socioeconomic backgrounds.
  • Drove community engagement by hosting guest speakers from diverse computer science fields, providing students with exposure to varied career paths and answering their questions, thereby bridging the opportunity gap.
  • Empowered students from diverse backgrounds to explore computer science, cultivating skills in problem-solving, logical thinking, and collaboration through hands-on projects, including currently programming robots
  • Cultivated a culture of technological literacy and self-belief, transforming student perceptions from technology consumers to creators, regardless of prior experience or socioeconomic status.
